# 🎨 Vecina Digital Illustration System

> Every illustration should feel like it belongs to the same little neighbourhood.
>
> Not because they share colours.
>
> Because they share a soul.

---

# Purpose

Illustrations are not decoration.

They create emotional safety.

Someone visiting Vecina Digital should feel:

> "I'm welcome here."

Before reading a single word.

Every illustration should reduce distance.

Not create it.

---

# The Neighbourhood

Vecina Digital is not just a brand.

It is a tiny neighbourhood.

Every illustration belongs somewhere inside it.

Every house.

Every computer.

Every shop.

Every sign.

Every flower pot.

Every object feels like it knows the others.

Nothing exists in isolation.

Every icon belongs to the neighbourhood.

---

# Living Objects

Objects are never merely objects.

Technology has feelings.

Houses have memories.

Doors welcome people.

Plants quietly celebrate life.

Every illustration should feel like it could have a name.

If you can imagine saying:

> "Good morning."

to the illustration...

it belongs in Vecina Digital.

# Artistic Direction

Every illustration should feel hand painted.

Never AI-looking.

Never perfectly clean.

Never hyper realistic.

Brush texture is welcome.

Paper texture is welcome.

Natural variation is welcome.

Imperfection is part of the charm.

---

# Materials

Everything should feel touchable.

Wood feels carved.

Stone feels warm.

Clay feels handmade.

Paper feels textured.

Plants feel alive.

Nothing should feel synthetic.

Nothing should feel disposable.

---

# Line Work

Medium thickness.

Soft warm-brown outlines.

Never pure black.

Never vector-perfect.

Natural variation in width.

Rounded corners.

Confident strokes.

---

# Light

Golden afternoon light.

Soft shadows.

Quiet atmosphere.

Never dramatic.

Never high contrast.

# Composition

One clear subject.

Lots of breathing room.

Simple first.

Beautiful second.

Complexity only when it serves emotion.

Remove before adding.

If a detail doesn't make someone feel something...

remove it.

# Iconicity

Every illustration should be recognisable in silhouette.

Simple enough to remember.

Detailed enough to feel alive.

Readable even at small sizes.

Never visually noisy.

# The Storybook Test

Before approving any illustration, ask:

- Would this illustration belong in the same storybook as the Door?
- Would a child want to touch it?
- Would a local bakery owner feel welcome?
- Would it still look beautiful printed on textured paper?
- Could it exist somewhere inside the Vecina Digital neighbourhood?

If not...

Redraw it.

---

# The Door Principle

Every illustration should answer one question:

> "If this object had a little door...

> ...would I want to knock on it?"

If the answer is yes...

The illustration belongs in Vecina Digital.
