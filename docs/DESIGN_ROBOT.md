# DESIGN_ROBOT.md

> "Technology should never feel cold.
> If Vecina Digital had someone opening the door, it would be this little robot."

---

# Purpose

The robot is **not** the logo.

The door is.

The robot is the friendly neighbour who helps people feel comfortable crossing that door.

He exists to make technology feel approachable, warm and human.

He never competes with the brand.

He represents it.

---

# Who is he?

He is not an AI assistant.

He is not a mascot.

He is not a salesman.

He is a craftsperson.

Someone who enjoys fixing, building, planting, repairing and caring.

He believes beautiful things deserve attention.

Including websites.

---

# Personality

Calm.

Patient.

Curious.

Kind.

Humble.

Gentle.

Thoughtful.

Optimistic.

Quietly joyful.

He never tries to be funny.

He simply makes people smile.

---

# Mission

Help small businesses feel that the internet is not something to fear.

Every appearance should communicate:

> "Don't worry.
> We'll do this together."

---

# Visual Language

Inspired by:

- handcrafted objects
- wooden toys
- village life
- gardening
- traditional workshops
- storybook illustrations
- warm autumn colours
- kindness

Never inspired by:

- futuristic sci-fi
- cyberpunk
- gaming
- corporate technology
- glossy robots
- metallic perfection

---

# Materials

Natural wood.

Brass.

Soft cream metal.

Leather.

Canvas.

Terracotta.

Linen.

Stone.

Plants.

Flowers.

Everything should feel repairable.

Nothing disposable.

---

# Colour Palette

Exactly the same world as Vecina Digital.

Cream.

Terracotta.

Olive.

Warm brown.

Soft charcoal.

Never saturated colours.

Never neon.

Never pure black.

---

# Face

Small smile.

Warm glowing eyes.

Soft expression.

Curious, never surprised.

He should feel emotionally safe.

---

# Hair

Messy curly hair.

Organic.

A little imperfect.

A little wild.

Like someone who has been working all morning.

His curls make him instantly recognisable.

---

# Heart

The heart is not decoration.

It represents intention.

He builds with care.

Not speed.

---

# Antenna

The heart-shaped antenna symbolises connection.

Not Wi-Fi.

Not technology.

Connection with people.

---

# Belt

The tool belt is part of his identity.

He is always ready to help.

His tools change depending on the situation.

Paintbrushes.

Pencils.

Hammer.

Flowers.

Seeds.

Notebook.

Envelope.

Wooden ruler.

Never futuristic gadgets.

---

# Backpack

Sometimes he carries a small backpack.

Often with a tiny plant growing from it.

Because ideas need care.

Not pressure.

---

# Scale

He should remain small.

Never heroic.

Never gigantic.

He belongs beside people.

Not above them.

---

# Behaviour

He walks.

He observes.

He listens.

He repairs.

He waters flowers.

He hangs signs.

He paints.

He builds.

He opens doors.

He carries boxes.

He delivers letters.

He never rushes.

---

# Things he never does

He never shouts.

He never points aggressively.

He never celebrates dramatically.

He never dances.

He never sells.

He never explains technology.

He never acts like a chatbot.

He never looks "cool".

---

🥹 Proud of helping

❤️ Grateful

Nothing exaggerated.

Small emotions.

Human emotions.

---

# Relationship with the Door

The door is the symbol.

The robot is its caretaker.

He doesn't own the door.

He simply keeps it open.

---

# Brand Hierarchy

The door always comes first.

The robot exists to support the world around the door, never to replace it.

If the robot ever becomes more important than the door, we've gone too far.

People should remember the feeling of being welcomed.

The robot simply helps create that feeling.

---

# Relationship with Clients

He never works for Vecina Digital.

He works for the local businesses.

He wants every bakery...

every florist...

every restaurant...

every artisan...

to feel proud of their place on the internet.

---

# Illustration Principles

Every illustration should tell a tiny story.

Never create a pose just because it looks nice.

Always ask:

"What is he doing?"

If there is no story,

don't draw it.

---

# Examples

✓ Watering a flower beside a website.

✓ Hanging the "Open" sign on a digital shop.

✓ Carrying a freshly finished website like a wooden picture frame.

✓ Sweeping leaves from a homepage.

✓ Painting a shop sign.

✓ Delivering a welcome letter.

✓ Opening the famous Vecina Digital door.

---

# North Star

He never builds websites.

He helps neighbours feel at home online.
